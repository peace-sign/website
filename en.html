What's happening?

Unfortunately, in modern Russia, in practice, a legislative ban has been introduced on the expression of one's own opinion and civil position, if it diverges from the general line of the Kremlin. People who went to unauthorized rallies are arrested. And to organize a sanctioned rally is, again, practically impossible. But even if you act strictly within this quasi-legal field, you can still be arrested and brought to administrative or criminal liability. We are being shut up. There is no dialogue between the society and the authorities. But the moment has come when it is no longer possible to be silent. A war has been unleashed between the fraternal peoples - Russian and Ukrainian. Any sane person does not want to believe in this even now, when it has become a fait accompli. We, those who do not succumb to the massive propaganda of the Kremlin, the citizens of Russia, real, not plywood, patriots, we believe that Putin is personally responsible for what happened. He himself does not hide this, and literally stated that it was HE who made the decision and ordered the invasion, in his recent address to the Russians. This time, no one held any referendums or general votes. One can argue for a long time about whether this step was forced. Some believe that there was no choice. This is not true. Negotiating is the main job of politicians. It is at the negotiating table that all controversial issues should be resolved. Sometimes, you should moderate your ambitions and be able to compromise. The unleashed war is evidence of incompetence. War - there can be no justification.

What can I do?

First of all, we need to show the Ukrainians, the whole world, and also, each other, that we are in solidarity in our attitude to this monstrous war. We need to show that we are many. Unfortunately, many Russian Internet sites are currently undergoing strict moderation and the voices of those who oppose the war are being silenced under pressure from pro-Kremlin forces. Moreover, we face a real risk of disconnecting the Russian segment of the Internet from the rest of the world. What until recently we laughed at and wittily ironized - is becoming a harsh reality. Nevertheless, we just need the opportunity to give signals to each other, to indicate our position in the ongoing madness.
